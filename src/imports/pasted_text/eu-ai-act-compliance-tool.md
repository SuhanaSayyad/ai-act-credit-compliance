Questionnaire Page
Design a multi-step questionnaire page for "EU AI Act Compliance Tool". The design should feel like a clean, structured intake form from a premium legal or regulatory service. Calm, focused, nothing distracting. No em dashes anywhere, use commas or periods instead.

Typography: IBM Plex Serif for headings, IBM Plex Sans for body and labels, IBM Plex Mono for step codes, field IDs, and small tags. Generous line height and spacing throughout.

Colors: navy #0A0E1A for the sidebar. White #FFFFFF for the main form area. Very light grey #F8F9FA for the page background and card footer. Blue #1E40AF for active step indicators and progress. Green #047857 for completed steps. No gradients, no shadows.

Layout: fixed left sidebar and main content area to the right. Lots of internal padding everywhere.

Left sidebar, navy background, wide enough to feel substantial not cramped. Top section: a small mono label "ASSESSMENT FORM" in muted white, then a serif heading "EU AI Act Compliance" with a mono subtitle "Five steps to five reports". Below that, a clean vertical list of five steps with generous spacing between each. Each step shows a small square step indicator with the step number, a bold step title, and a short one-line description underneath in small muted mono font. Current step: white square indicator with navy number, white title text, bright. Completed steps: green square indicator with checkmark, dimmed white title. Upcoming steps: outlined square indicator, very dimmed text. Thin hairline dividers between steps. At the bottom of the sidebar, after a lot of whitespace, a small note in very small mono font about session privacy.

Main area, white card centered on light grey background, generous margins on all sides. Card has no border radius or very minimal. Inside the card: a header area with a small mono tag "STEP 2 OF 5" in blue, below that a very thin progress bar the full width of the card, then a large serif heading for the step title "Data Processing" with a calm one-line subtitle below it. Generous padding before the form fields begin.

Form fields, well spaced with room to breathe between each one. Each field has a small mono field ID like "2.1" aligned to the left, a clean bold label in sans font, and a subtle question mark icon for help. Below the label, a clean full-width input box with a thin bottom border only rather than a full box border, giving it an editorial form feel. Two of the fields are yes and no toggle questions showing two adjacent pill buttons, navy when selected and white with a thin border when not selected.

Card footer, light grey background, thin top border, generous padding. A clean "Previous" text button on the left with a left arrow, and a solid navy "Continue" button on the right. IBM Plex Mono font for both.

Overall feel: calm, focused, nothing competing for attention. The user feels like they are filling in an important official document, not a consumer app survey.

Results Page
Design a results dashboard page for "EU AI Act Compliance Tool" showing five compliance assessment reports. The design should feel like a premium regulatory audit document presented digitally. Authoritative, clean, spacious, nothing cluttered. No em dashes anywhere, use commas or periods instead.

Typography: IBM Plex Serif for all report headings and the system name. IBM Plex Sans for body text and descriptions. IBM Plex Mono for labels, codes, status values, article references, and all technical data. Generous spacing throughout.

Colors: white #FFFFFF for the main content. Very light grey #F8F9FA for the page background and alternating rows. Navy #0A0E1A for the document header strip and section headers. Five article accent colors: blue #1E40AF for Article 27 FRIA, red #B91C1C for Article 15 Cybersecurity, green #047857 for Article 13 Explainability, amber #B45309 for Article 9 Risk, purple #6D28D9 for Article 10(5) Bias. Status colors: red background for HIGH, amber background for MEDIUM, green background for LOW, each as a small clean pill with a colored dot. No gradients, no shadows, flat throughout.

Layout top to bottom:

1. Document header strip, navy background, slim and authoritative. Left side: a small "Back to Assessment" link in mono font with a left arrow. Right side: small mono text showing the date and a version reference. Below that on white background: a large serif heading showing the system name that was assessed, a small mono subtitle with the regulation reference "Regulation 2024/1689 / Annex III Point 5(b)", and on the far right a clean navy "Export Full Report" button.

2. Compliance status overview, white background, generous padding. A small mono label "COMPLIANCE STATUS" centered. Below that five clean cells in a horizontal row separated by thin hairline borders, one per module. Each cell has a colored article reference in mono font at the top, the module name below it in small sans font, and a large status value in mono font colored by risk level at the bottom. A thin colored bar at the very bottom of each cell in the article accent color. Lots of internal padding in each cell.

3. Report tab navigation, white background, sticky below the header. Five tabs spaced generously. Each tab shows a small mono section number, the report name in sans font, and the article reference in tiny mono font below it. Active tab has a two pixel underline in the article accent color and the report name in that accent color. Inactive tabs are grey. Clean hairline bottom border on the whole tab bar.

4. Active report content area, light grey page background, generous padding. Report header: a small mono label showing the section and article reference, a large serif heading for the report name, a calm description line, and on the right a status pill badge and three small clean download buttons labeled PDF, CSV, JSON in mono font with thin borders.

Below that, a section label in small mono uppercase. Then a series of clean report cards, each with a thin colored left border matching the risk level, generous internal padding, the right or finding name in bold serif, the status badge on the right, a description paragraph, an italicised justification line below that, and a clean mitigation block at the bottom with a small mono "MITIGATION" label and explanation text. Good vertical spacing between each card.

Below that, another mono section label. Then a clean numbered list of recommendations, each with a small square number badge in navy and the recommendation text beside it. Generous spacing between each item.

Overall feel: feels like reading a premium audit report or legal compliance certificate. Every element has room to breathe. Nothing cramped. Professional enough to hand to a regulator.
